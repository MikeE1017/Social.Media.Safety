Social Media Safety

Recognizing Social Media Risks

Common Online Threats

Many threats exist on social media, but recognizing them helps you avoid problems before they happen.

  • Phishing: Fake messages or links designed to steal your login info.
  • Catfishing: People pretending to be someone else online.
  • Malware: Harmful software spread through suspicious links.
  • Oversharing: Accidentally revealing private details.

Good Habits for Safer Social Media Use

Practicing these habits daily will help protect your accounts and personal data:

  1. Pause before accepting new friend or follow requests.
  2. Check links carefully before clicking on them.
  3. Keep your apps, browsers, and devices updated.
  4. Teach friends and family about online scams so they can stay safe too.
What to Do If You're Targeted
  • Block and report abusive users.
  • Change your passwords immediately.
  • Enable two-factor authentication.
Report It
  1. Document the incident.
  2. Inform the platform's support team.
  3. Tell a trusted adult or authority if needed.
